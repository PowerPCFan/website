All About the Commodore 64
The Commodore 64 (C64) is an 8-bit home computer that was released in 1982. It is the best-selling single computer model of all time, with estimates placing the number of units sold between 12.5 and 17 million. The C64 was popular for its low price, its large library of games and software, and its powerful graphics and sound capabilities.

The C64 was designed by a team of engineers at Commodore, led by Chuck Peddle. The computer was based on the MOS Technology 6502 microprocessor, which was also used in the Apple II and Atari 8-bit computers. The C64 had 64 kilobytes of RAM, which was a lot of memory for a home computer at the time. It also had a built-in speaker and a joystick port.

The C64 was released in the United States in August 1982 for $595. It was an immediate success, and it quickly became the best-selling home computer in the world. The C64 was popular with both home users and businesses. It was used for word processing, spreadsheets, games, and programming.

The C64 was discontinued in 1994, but it remains a popular computer among retrocomputing enthusiasts. There are many emulators and other software tools available that allow people to use the C64 today.

Here are some of the key features of the Commodore 64:

64 kilobytes of RAM
Built-in speaker
Joystick port
Video output for a TV or monitor
Audio output for speakers or headphones
BASIC programming language
Large library of games and software


The Commodore 64 was a revolutionary computer that changed the way people used computers. It was one of the first affordable home computers that was powerful enough to be used for both business and entertainment. The C64 is still popular today, and it is considered to be one of the most important computers of all time.

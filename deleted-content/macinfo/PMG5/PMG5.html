All About the Power Mac G5
The Power Mac G5 was a series of personal computers designed, manufactured, and sold by Apple Computer from 2003 to 2006. It was the most powerful computer in Apple's Macintosh lineup at the time, and was marketed as the world's first 64-bit desktop computer. It was also the first desktop computer from Apple to use an anodized aluminum alloy enclosure.

Three generations of the Power Mac G5 were released before it was discontinued as part of the Mac transition to Intel processors. The Mac Pro, which replaced the Power Mac G5, retained a variation of the G5's enclosure design for seven more years.

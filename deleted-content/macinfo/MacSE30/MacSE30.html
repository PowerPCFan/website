All About the Macintosh SE/30
The Macintosh SE/30 was a personal computer made by Apple from 1989 to 1991.

It was the fastest of the original black-and-white compact Macintosh computers. The SE/30 had a 9-inch black-and-white screen and a single Processor Direct Slot, which could be used for third-party accelerators, network cards, or a display adapter.

The SE/30 could be upgraded to have up to 128 MB of RAM, which was a lot of RAM at the time. It came with a 40 or 80 MB hard drive.

It was also the first compact Mac to come with a 1.44 MB high density floppy disk drive as standard. The SE/30 was a popular computer, and it was used by many people for both work and personal use. It was powerful for its time, and it was able to run many different types of software.

The SE/30 was also a very reliable computer, and it was known for its durability. The SE/30 was an important computer in the history of Apple, and it helped to solidify the company's position as a leader in the personal computer industry. It was a well-designed and well-made computer, and it was a pleasure to use.
